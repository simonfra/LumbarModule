Introduction to teaching

Material for the teaching physio

Welcome. We hope you enjoy using these teaching modules and thank you for taking part in this trail. Your feedback with all that follows will help us significantly to ensure further refinement for the benefit of all who will come to use them. The following is a summary of what you will find and how to use the modules.

  • Library.  The library aims to provide background information specific to each module.  You will find anatomy, physiology and pathology sections, as well as a fourth category called ‘clinical reason’ which shows a flow of the clinical reasoning behind each and every diagnosis. 
  •  
  • Next is SOAP.  This reflects the patient treatment notes.  You’ll see in the objective section, a student is guided through an interactive assessment, to arrive at a diagnosis, which then leads onto a treatment plan.  Students can use this as a teaching tool and be left with it to use in practice after you have left their location.
  •  
  • Student Extras.  Within this section you will find:
    • SOAP pdf for printing out and also a guide on how to teach patient record management (in development).  This is crucial to the accountability and therefore sustainability of the clinic.
    • Outcome measures. 
    • Accountibility.  A sheet to print out for your student provide statistics to their stakeholders. 
    • Teaching games.  Here you will find three yellow boxes on the top left.  The middle one represents the pre/post teaching test.  (See below).

We recommend teaching in this order:

  • 1. Starting with the knowledge test (within student extras), explain to the student that it is vital for DevelopingPhysio to understand their background learning, so that by completing the test, their learning can be geared to the appropriate need.  Make a note of the student's score percentage.
  • 2. Teaching can then begin, using the Library (Anatomy, Physiology, Pathology and Assessment Steps).
  • 3. This is followed by teaching the student about SOAP notes (within student extras).
  • 4. The student is then ready to poceed to the SOAP section and can be taught how to use the interactive assessment to arrive at a diagnosis followed by an effective treatment and plan.  The teaching games may prove useful at this stage.
  • 5. The student exam forms the next stage and, to prepare for this, we recommend pointing out to the student that there are sections for outcome measures, accountability, further reading and support material.
  • 6. Then take the student through the exam to ensure that they can make a correct diagnosis and safe/effective treatment plan. They then repeat the knowledge test done at the start whilst you make a note of their score.

Please could you then fill in the supervision questionnaire (here)
And finally the Teacher’s questionnaire (here)

  Thank you for all feedback, both positive and negative, this will provide essential details for us to refine the module further.
Kind regards,
The DP team.
